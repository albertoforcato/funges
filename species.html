Wild Edibles on Funges

These are the wild plants, berries, nuts and mushrooms currently featured on the map. Each has specific growing conditions, and their presence is inferred from environmental data.

Amaranth

Edible plant with green or red leaves and small seeds. Young leaves are eaten raw or cooked. Seeds are collected and used like grains.

Asparagus (Wild)

Thin green shoots that grow from perennial roots. Shoots are collected in spring before they become woody. Grows near shrubs, trails, and edges of forests.

Black Chanterelle

Dark gray to black mushroom with a funnel shape. Grows on forest floors among leaf litter, especially in deciduous woods. Appears in late summer and autumn.

Chestnut

Brown edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. Ripen in autumn and fall from the tree. Often collected from the ground in chestnut woodlands.

Chickweed

Low-growing plant with oval leaves and small white flowers. Young leaves and stems are edible. Common in gardens and damp soils.

Dandelion

Widespread plant with jagged leaves, yellow flowers, and deep roots. Leaves are used fresh or cooked. Roots are harvested in autumn.

Garlic (Wild)

Also called ramsons. Has broad, lance-shaped green leaves and white flowers. Smells like garlic. Found in damp, shaded forests.

Larch Cones

Small cones from the larch tree. Collected for extract or syrup. Larch is the only deciduous conifer in Europe, with needle drop in autumn.

Lingonberry

Evergreen shrub producing small red berries. Berries are tart and used in preserves. Grows in acidic soils of boreal forests.

Masterwort

Herbaceous plant native to mountainous areas. Has toothed leaves and white or pink umbrella-like flower heads. Historically used in alpine cooking.

Morel

Mushroom with a cone-shaped, honeycomb-textured cap. Grows in moist forests and clearings after spring rains. Hollow inside.

Porcini

Large mushroom with thick white stalk and brown cap. Found near conifer and broadleaf trees. Cap is smooth, pores underneath instead of gills.

Nettle

Herbaceous plant with serrated leaves and stinging hairs. Leaves are edible when cooked or dried. Found in nutrient-rich soils.

Parasol Mushroom

Large mushroom with a tall stem and a wide, scaly cap. Found in grassy areas, forest edges, and clearings in late summer and autumn.

Raspberry

Shrub with woody stems and thorny branches. Produces red aggregate fruits in summer. Common in sunny clearings and disturbed areas.

Sorrel

Perennial herb with arrow-shaped leaves. Tastes sour due to oxalic acid. Used in soups, sauces, and salads.

Strawberry (Wild)

Small plant with runners and trifoliate leaves. Produces tiny red fruits with a strong aroma. Found in sunny slopes and woodland edges.

Truffle (Black)

Fungus that grows underground in symbiosis with tree roots. Collected using trained animals. Found in calcareous soils of southern Europe.

Walnut

Tree producing green husks that contain brown-shelled nuts. Nuts are collected in autumn after falling. Common in woodlands and valleys.
